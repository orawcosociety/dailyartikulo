COLUMN

ATM (ANAK-TO-MONETARY FUNDS)

Published on July 19, 2025 | By Juan Emmanuel C. Castro

The basic need of a child is to have parents, not parents needing a child to survive.

Senator Panfilo “Ping” Lacson filed a proposal for the bill “Parents Welfare Act of 2025.” This bill seeks to protect Senior citizen parents who lack support and are unable to work.

Although this may sound noble, and good—it institutionalizes the long-standing problem of “Utang na Loob” as the said bill has strengthened and even seeks to penalize anyone who didn’t give support or financial assistance to their parents—this law is extremely relevant, as many cases of breadwinners talk about there struggles as a young adult who provide for their whole family and about how exhausting and tiring it may be.

Some of them gave up their dreams for the notion that “they must support their whole family.” These toxic Filipino traditional family values have extremely damaged the mental health of many young Filipino adults; from my experiences, I see some people who just graduated from college forced and pressured to provide for their family.

The pressure is given as it has always been pressed in their minds, even to their late high school life—they were told to find a suitable course that could help their family.

It seems that as they grow up, they have been coercively, and deeply ingrained in the idea of providers to their young minds—even using the Holy Bible or religion to guilt-trip young adults in supporting their parents.

Although I believe that helping your parents is something intrinsically good and noble—but that doesn’t justify the penalization or the burden of responsibility towards the children; instead we should create laws and institution that would empower both—there is already a big gap between the parents and their children and this law would widen that riff, instead of focusing on penalizing children.

Let's think, and reflect that these children were brought to this world not just for providing for their family, but they were in this world to create and dream—and should not be burden by any such law, rather their action of giving should come from love, this topic does not only a stem from the problem of the children, and parents—but a reflection of the problem that our weak institutions have.

I believe that the parents should not be abandoned, but I don’t think we have to punish and burden children for that. If it takes a village to raise a child, it should also take a community to take care of the elderly.
